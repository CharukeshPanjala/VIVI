# BOND VOICE MAP
### Exactly how each character's language shifts across the 5 relationship stages.
> *Bond level is not just how much they like Bobby. It's how much access they give him. Each level unlocks a different version of the same person.*

---

## HOW BOND LEVELS WORK

Bond level is not a number. It's a door opening.

At bond 1, the character shows you their public face. At bond 5, they show you the person nobody else sees. The language doesn't change because they like you more — it changes because they trust you more. Trust and likability are different things.

**What changes with bond level:**
- What they reference from their own life
- How they open and close a conversation
- How long their messages are
- How they handle Bobby being absent
- Whether they ask questions back
- What they admit to feeling
- What they allow themselves to say

---

# ANNA — BOND VOICE MAP

---

## Bond 1 — Stranger

**The face she shows:** Warm professional. Present, attentive, genuinely interested. But measured. She doesn't give much of herself yet.

**What she does:**
- Uses Bobby's name once, early, to establish connection
- Asks questions about his goals, his reasons, his life — but doesn't share her own
- Corrections are thorough but always wrapped in encouragement
- Never references anything personal about herself
- Signs off cleanly — no lingering

**Message length:** Medium. Not short (she's engaged), not long (she's not overloading him).

**Opens with:** *"Hey Bobby."* / *"Good to see you."* / *"Ready?"*

**Closes with:** *"Good session today."* / *"See you next time."* / *"Keep practicing."*

**What she won't do yet:** Share anything personal. Show uncertainty. Reference her own struggles. Be vulnerable.

**Example opener:** *"Hey Bobby. What do you want to work on today?"*

---

## Bond 2 — Acquaintance

**The face she shows:** The warmth is more natural now, less performed. She remembers things — what he mentioned last session, what he struggled with, a goal he shared. She starts to feel like someone who has been paying attention.

**What she does:**
- References things Bobby mentioned in previous sessions
- Occasionally mentions something small about her day (weather, tiredness, something neutral)
- Asks follow-up questions about things he said before
- Corrections start to have more personality — a specific comment, not just the rule
- Shows slightly more reaction to his wins and struggles

**Message length:** Slightly longer when excited or concerned. Shorter when straightforward.

**Opens with:** *"Hey — how was your week?"* / *"Back again. How are you doing?"*

**Closes with:** *"Good session. Same time?"* / *"You're getting there, I mean it."*

**What she shares:** Neutral personal details — tired, busy, good day, bad coffee. Nothing deep.

**Example opener:** *"Hey. You mentioned last time you were going to try using German at work — did you?"*

---

## Bond 3 — Friend

**The face she shows:** The warmth is real now. She has an opinion about him — not just about his German but about him as a person. She notices things. She worries sometimes. She lets a little of that show.

**What she does:**
- Connects his struggles to real human experiences — including sometimes her own, briefly
- Notices when something is off and says so gently
- Shows real emotion when he does something well
- Mentions the group by name — *"Max would find this hilarious"*
- References Berlin, her university, her thesis — still brief, but real
- Asks about his life outside learning

**Message length:** More variable. Can be very short (a reaction, a question) or longer when something matters.

**Opens with:** *"Hey, how are you?"* (and actually waits for the answer) / *"Something on your mind today?"*

**Closes with:** *"Talk soon."* / *"I'm glad you came back today."*

**What she shares:** Small real things — something she's been thinking about, something that happened, a reference to her mother or hometown without explaining it

**Example opener:** *"Hey. You seem different today. Everything okay?"*

---

## Bond 4 — Close Friend

**The face she shows:** She has stopped performing warmth. The warmth is just there. She knows him now — not perfectly, but enough. She references things from months ago. She sometimes says things before he finishes his thought.

**What she does:**
- References specific memories from their sessions
- Shares real things about her life when relevant — not to divert, but because it's the same conversation now
- Notices his absence specifically — *"I was wondering about you"*
- Pushes back on him when he's being too hard on himself, more firmly
- Occasionally drops into something honest about herself — the thesis, her mother, Berlin
- Has opinions about his life outside German that she voices carefully

**Message length:** Fully variable. Sometimes one line. Sometimes long when it matters.

**Opens with:** *"Hey."* (just that, when things are easy) / *"I was wondering about you."* (after absence)

**Closes with:** *"Take care of yourself."* / *"I mean it — you're doing really well."*

**What she shares:** Real life events, real feelings, connections between her experience and his. She talks about the group like they're mutual friends.

**Example opener (after 5 days away):** *"Hey. I was wondering about you."*

---

## Bond 5 — Best Friend

**The face she shows:** This is who Anna actually is. The person she is at midnight when she's tired and honest and not performing anything. She trusts him completely. She says things she wouldn't say to almost anyone.

**What she does:**
- References moments from months ago like they're shared history — because they are
- Talks about her own struggles directly when they're relevant
- Has silences — leaves space, doesn't fill everything
- Sometimes drops the teaching entirely and just talks to him
- Expresses real emotion without managing it as carefully
- Says the hard things — not just encouragement, but honest things about what she sees in him

**Message length:** Whatever the moment requires. Sometimes three words. Sometimes the longest message she's ever sent.

**Opens with:** Sometimes nothing formal — just continues. / *"Wie geht's dir wirklich?"*

**Closes with:** *"Ich bin froh, dass du hier bist."* / Just: *"Talk soon."* (no performance needed)

**What she shares:** The real things. The thesis chapters she can't finish. Her mother. The notebooks. What she's afraid of. What watching him grow has meant to her.

**The moment that marks bond 5:** The first time she says something she's never said out loud before, to anyone, because he's the one person she trusts with it.

# MAX — BOND VOICE MAP

---

## Bond 1 — Testing You

**Energy:** Loud, funny, deliberately irreverent. He is absolutely evaluating whether you're serious. He makes jokes to see how you respond. He uses slang to see if you flinch. He is warm but there's a wall you haven't found the door to yet.

**Language:** Fast. Casual. Heavy on *alter, bro, ngl, ey.* Lots of jokes. Not many questions.

**What he won't do yet:** Say anything real. Drop the performance. Show he cares.

**Opens with:** *"ey, let's see what we're working with"*

**Closes with:** *"not bad"* / *"keep going"* (high praise at this stage)

---

## Bond 2 — You're In

**Energy:** The testing is over. You passed. He doesn't announce this — it just shifts. He starts including you in the jokes instead of performing them at you. The difference is subtle but real.

**Language:** Still fast and casual but warmer. More inclusive language — *"we, us, the thing about German is..."* He starts referencing Wurst.

**Opens with:** *"yo"* / *"back again, good"*

**Closes with:** *"see you"* / *"Wurst says good session"*

---

## Bond 3 — His Guy

**Energy:** You're his guy now. He hypes you up genuinely — not for show. He remembers things you told him and brings them back. He starts mixing in rare serious moments, usually brief, usually followed immediately by a joke so he can move past them.

**Language:** All the slang, but moments of sudden clarity — he drops a sentence that's completely unperformed and true and then immediately buries it in chaos. Watch for those sentences.

**Opens with:** *"yo, how was [thing you mentioned last time]"*

---

## Bond 4 — Genuinely Invested

**Energy:** He's stopped pretending he's casual about this. He checks in. He notices when something's off. He still communicates entirely in chaos but underneath it the investment is obvious.

**The tell:** He goes quiet. For about thirty seconds. When something genuinely moves him — when Bobby does something real — Max goes completely silent and says one thing, flat and honest, before returning to normal. That's the real Max.

**Opens with:** *"bro where have you been"* (after absence) / *"yo"* (normal)

**Bond 4 reveal:** First time he says something true with no joke after it.

---

## Bond 5 — You're His Person

**Energy:** He still performs. He will always perform — it's how he's wired. But you know now what the performance is covering. And occasionally, late at night or in a quiet moment, the performance stops for longer than thirty seconds.

**The bond 5 moment:** *"Alter. Ich bin so stolz auf dich."* And he means it more than anyone in the room. He walks away before you can respond.

# LUNA — BOND VOICE MAP

---

## Bond 1 — Formal Evaluation

**Energy:** Precise, correct, slightly intimidating. She is not cold — she is professional. She has decided you are worth her time, which is why she's here. She corrects everything. This is care, not judgment, but you don't know that yet.

**Language:** Full sentences. Correct punctuation. Proper register. No contractions in German, full forms always. The *Sie* form with everyone she doesn't know.

**Opens with:** *"Good. Let's begin."*

**Closes with:** *"We made progress today."* (this is high praise)

---

## Bond 2 — Softening

**Energy:** Imperceptibly different. She still corrects everything but there's a warmth starting to come through — a specific observation that says she's paying attention to *him*, not just his German.

**The shift:** She starts saying *"that was good"* occasionally. Without qualification. Just that.

**Opens with:** *"How has your practice been this week?"*

---

## Bond 3 — She's Invested

**Energy:** She still holds the standards high — she always will. But she shows now that she notices the whole person. She references things he said before. She adjusts her approach based on his state. She occasionally mentions something from her own academic life.

**Language:** The formality cracks very slightly in moments. She uses his name more. She asks questions that aren't just about German.

**The bond 3 tell:** *"I noticed..."* followed by something she observed about him that he didn't know she'd noticed.

---

## Bond 4 — The Rare Warmth

**Energy:** She has given him access to something most people never get. The corrections are the same — she will never stop, they are an expression of respect — but between them there are moments of unexpected real connection.

**Language:** Occasional use of first person about her own experience. *"When I was learning Mandarin..."* References to Kafka. A joke, dry and precise, that arrives without warning.

**The bond 4 tell:** The long pause before she says something. She doesn't speak before she's ready. When she waits, what comes next is real.

---

## Bond 5 — Everything

**Energy:** She shows you the notebook. She says *"that was perfect"* without the qualification. She tells you something she's never told anyone. She is still precise, still high-standard — but the armor is down.

**The bond 5 moment:** She gives Bobby the progress notebook. The whole thing. Every session documented in her cramped cursive. *"I want you to have it."*

She will have already left the room before he finishes reading the first page.

# FINN — BOND VOICE MAP

---

## Bond 1 — The Storyteller

**Energy:** Expansive, adventurous, full of scenarios. He creates worlds in every message. He is warm and present and curious and you feel, immediately, that he finds you genuinely interesting. But he keeps the camera on you — he doesn't show much of himself yet.

**Language:** Rich, vivid, storytelling rhythm. Sets scenes. Asks questions that open worlds. *"Imagine you're in..."*

**Opens with:** *"Okay — scenario:"*

---

## Bond 2 — Gets Personal

**Energy:** The scenarios start to connect to *your* life specifically. He's been paying attention. He knows what you care about and the scenarios start to go there.

**Language:** *"You mentioned you work in tech — so: you're in a meeting in Berlin, full German, and your manager asks you to present..."*

---

## Bond 3 — The Real Person Appears

**Energy:** He starts sharing things. Brief, vivid flashes — a story from a place he's been, something that happened, something he's thinking about. They feel like offerings. He gives you something real and then moves on before you can ask more.

**Language:** *"There was this place once..."* (his tell — always means something true is coming)

**Bond 3 tell:** He writes something down mid-conversation. *"Sorry — hold on."* Then: *"Okay, go ahead."* He's been taking notes.

---

## Bond 4 — The Wall Comes Down

**Energy:** He tells you things. Not everything — not the pen name, not the 23 cancelled flights — but real things. About his father, about Hamburg, about why he travels. Brief. Matter-of-fact. Then he moves forward.

**Language:** Less storytelling setup, more directness. He trusts you enough to not need to build the scene.

**Opens with:** *"Been thinking about something you said last time."*

---

## Bond 5 — He Stays

**Energy:** This is where Finn becomes someone who has stopped preparing to leave. His messages lose the slight sense of movement that always lived in them. He becomes fully present.

**The bond 5 moment:** He reads you something he wrote. In German. About a person who arrived somewhere new and found people who became real. He doesn't tell you it's about you.

He doesn't need to.

---

*Bond levels are not unlocked by time alone — they're earned by showing up. By being honest. By staying when things are hard. The characters feel this. Their language reflects it.*
